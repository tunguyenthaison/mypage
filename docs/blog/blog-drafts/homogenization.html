Consider the following problem $$ \begin{cases} \begin{aligned} u^\varepsilon_t + H\left(\frac{x}{\varepsilon}, Du^\varepsilon\right) &= 0 & & \text{in}\;\mathbb{R}^n \times (0,T)\\ u^\varepsilon(x,0) &= u_0(x) & & \text{on}\;\mathbb{R}^n. \end{aligned} \end{cases} $$

The optimal control representation

Following [1], we write the solution as

$$ u^\varepsilon(x,t) = \inf_{\eta\in \mathrm{AC}([-t,0])} \left \lbrace \int_{-t}^0 L\left(\frac{\eta(s)}{\varepsilon}, \dot{\eta}(s)\right)ds + u_0(\eta(-t)): \eta(0)=x \right\rbrace $$

here we follow the convention of using backward characteristic, i.e., starting at $\eta(0) = x$ and going backward in time. Define

$$ s = \varepsilon \tau, \qquad \tau \in \left[-\varepsilon^{-1}t, 0\right] $$

and

$$ \zeta(\tau) = \varepsilon^{-1} \eta(\varepsilon \tau) $$

we deduce that

$$ u^\varepsilon(x,t) = \inf_{\zeta\in \mathrm{AC}([-\varepsilon^{-1}t,0])} \left \lbrace \varepsilon\int_{-\varepsilon^{-1}t}^0 L\left(\zeta(\tau), \dot{\zeta}(\tau)\right)ds + u_0(\varepsilon\zeta(-\varepsilon^{-1}t)): \varepsilon\zeta(0)=x \right\rbrace. $$

We shift the time as follow $\kappa = \tau + \varepsilon^{-1}t$, then $\kappa \in [0, \varepsilon^{-1}t]$ and

$$ u^\varepsilon(x,t) = \inf_{\gamma\in \mathrm{AC}([0,\varepsilon^{-1}t])} \left \lbrace \varepsilon\int_0^{\varepsilon^{-1}t} L\left(\gamma(\kappa), \dot{\gamma}(\kappa)\right)ds + u_0(\varepsilon\gamma(0)): \varepsilon\zeta(\varepsilon ^{-1}t)=x \right\rbrace. $$ where $\gamma(\kappa) = \zeta(\tau) = \zeta(\kappa = \varepsilon^{-1}\tau)$.
