The Cross Product of

\(\seteqnumber{0}{}{0}\)

\begin{equation*} \textbf {a} = a_1\textbf {i} + a_2\textbf {j} + a_3\textbf {k} = (a_1,a_2,a_3)\qquad \text {and}\qquad \textbf {b} = b_1\textbf {i} + b_2\textbf {j} + b_3\textbf {k}=(b_1,b_2,b_3) \end{equation*}

is a vector that is perpendicular to both \(\textbf {a}\) and \(\textbf {b}\).

\(\seteqnumber{0}{}{0}\)

\begin{equation*} \textbf {a} \times \textbf {b} = \left | \begin{array}{ccc} \textbf {i} & \textbf {j} & \textbf {k} \\ a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \end {array} \right | = \textbf {i} \underbrace { \left | \begin{array}{cc} a_2 & a_3 \\ b_2 & b_3 \end {array} \right | }_{a_2b_3-b_2a_3} - \textbf {j} \underbrace { \left | \begin{array}{cc} a_1 & a_3 \\ b_1 & b_3 \end {array} \right | }_{a_1b_3-b_1a_3} + \textbf {k} \underbrace { \left | \begin{array}{cc} a_1 & a_2 \\ b_1 & b_2 \end {array} \right | }_{a_1b_2-b_1a_2}. \end{equation*}

Example Evaluate \((1,2,3)\times (-2,1,0)\).

Notes and Theorem \(\textbf {a}\cdot \textbf {b}\) is a number, while \(\textbf {a}\times \textbf {b}\) is a vector.

  • (a) \(\textbf {a} \times \textbf {b}\) is both perpendicular to \(\textbf {a}\) and \(\textbf {b}\).

  • (b) If \(\theta \) is the angle between \(\textbf {a}\cdot \textbf {b}\) (\(0\leq \theta \leq \pi \)) then \(|\textbf {a}\cdot \textbf {b}| = |\textbf {a}|.|\textbf {b}|\sin \theta \).

  • (c) Two nonzero vectors \(\textbf {a}\) and \(\textbf {b}\) are parallel if and only if \(|\textbf {a}\times \textbf {b}| = 0\).

Example Find a vector \(\textbf {u}\) that satisfies \(\textbf {u}\cdot (9,3,1) = 0\) and \(\textbf {u}\cdot (-2,4,0) = 0\).

Theorem - Direction of the Cross Product Take two non-zero non-parallel vectors \(\textbf {a}\) and \(\textbf {b}\). Then the direction of \(\textbf {a}\times \textbf {b}\) is determined by the right-hand rule. That is: the way your right thumb handrub right points when your right-hand fingers curl through the angle \(\theta \) from \(\textbf {a}\) to \(\textbf {b}\).

Computing area of triangle

.

Given a triangle \(ABC\), we have

\(\seteqnumber{0}{}{0}\)

\begin{equation*} S_{ABC} = \frac {1}{2}|\vec {AB}\times \vec {AC}| = \frac {1}{2}|AB|.|AC|.\sin (\theta ). \end{equation*}

Example Find the area of the triangle with vertices \(P(1, 0, 1)\), \(Q(-2, 1, 3)\), and \(R(4, 2, 5)\).
